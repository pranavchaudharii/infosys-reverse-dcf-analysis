## 🔍 Project Overview

This project performs a **Reverse Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) analysis** on Infosys to evaluate what growth expectations are currently priced into the stock.

The objective is to bridge the gap between **market expectations and actual financial performance** using structured financial modeling and valuation techniques.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Derive **market-implied Free Cash Flow (FCF) growth**
* Compare implied growth with **historical financial performance**
* Evaluate **valuation vs fundamentals**
* Assess whether the stock is **overvalued, fairly priced, or undervalued**

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* 📌 Implied FCF Growth: **~11%**
* 📌 Revenue Growth (CAGR): **~9.2%**
* 📌 EPS Growth (CAGR): **~5.2%**

**Interpretation:**
The market appears to be pricing in **optimistic growth expectations**, slightly above historical trends. This suggests confidence in future performance, potentially driven by margin expansion and digital transformation demand.

---

## 💼 Valuation Approach

* Reverse DCF Model
* Free Cash Flow (FCF) Projections
* WACC Calculation (CAPM-based Cost of Equity)
* Terminal Value using **Gordon Growth Model**
* Sensitivity Analysis (Growth vs Discount Rate)
